Resources

Tutors & Counselors

Tutorial support is available from faculty and peer tutors in the MESA Student Study Center. These tutors are available on a drop in basis and help students with biology, engineering, computer science, chemistry, math and physics.

Tutors are avaliable in-person at the MESA center or online through MESA's Discord. Please visit the tutor's page for more information.

Counselors are avalible online via Zoom or in-person at the MESA Center. Please visit the counselor's page for more information.

Scholarships & Internships

These scholarships can be found thorough COC's website, and new scholarships are announced through MESA's Canvas.

New internships are announced through MESA's Canvas. Information is also avaliable on the MESA Student Internships bulletin board at the MESA Center.

Workshops & Programs

AEW is a study group program led by MESA's tutors to help students succeed in their classes. Groups are formed based around specific courses and professors in order to help students with their assignments and exams. Please see MESA's Canvas or ask the front desk for a schedule of this semester's AEWs.

The Peer-Mentor Program is designed to help newer MESA members connect with more experienced members. These students share their knowledge about being a student at COC. They can offer valuable insight on professors, applying to transfer, finding internships, and other resources. Please ask the MESA's front desk or contact Amy Foote for more information.

If you have a GPA of at least 3.0 and are eligible for financial aid, you may qualify for the Physics/Calculus Book Award Program! You may receive a free textbook if your are enrolled in Physics 220 OR Math 211. A limited number of awards are available so make sure to submit your application as soon as possible! This program is open to all STEM majors. For more information and an application, please visit the MESA Center.

*Only Fully-Qualified MESA Students eligible

Rentals

Textbooks are avaliable for students to use in the MESA Center. They are also avaliable for studnets to checkout overnight.

Textbooks for math, biology, chemistry, physics, and engineering are avaliable.

Graphing calculators are avalible for MESA students to rent out for the semester. For more information and an application, please visit the MESA Center.

Laptops are avalible for MESA students to rent out for the semester. For more information and an application, please visit the MESA Center.

MESA's Locker Rental Program provides students a safe place to store their belongings while attending class. Lockers are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis at the start of each semester.
If you would like to use a locker for the duration of the semester, please visit the MESA Center for an application.

*Only Fully-Qualified MESA Students eligible
